## Summary

Across ~9 working sessions you directed the AI through skill authoring, project
docs, the end-to-end app build, a UI refresh, and deterministic API test
scaffolding. Your collaboration style was **delegate-then-steer**: you gave a
high-level goal, approved a direction, then issued tight corrections when the
output missed your taste — most visibly in the UI work, where you rejected three
specific design choices in a row. You accepted most architecture/tooling
recommendations but did **not** accept everything, contrary to the earlier
`ai-suggestions-report.md`.

## How You Steered the AI

- **Front-loaded constraints:** e.g. "use this API key, do **not** configure CI yet, test locally first" — which is exactly why the AI chose the file-backed favorites fallback.
- **Gated big work behind brainstorming:** approved with explicit choices ("Approach 1", "1", "3", "go ahead", "yes") rather than open-ended go-aheads.
- **Iterated on UX in small, specific corrections** instead of re-specifying the whole UI.
- **Kept scope tight:** declined an unsolicited follow-up ("no") and asked for the interaction to be logged.
